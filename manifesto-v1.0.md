## We Were Burnt

We've been there.

The agencies that billed us by the hour and measured us by utilization rates. The startups that promised equity and delivered 80-hour weeks with no ownership. The corporations that bought our time and called it a career.

We felt our agency erode, meeting by meeting. Our craft reduced to tickets. Our ambition flattened by people who feared what we could become.

We watched brilliant entrepreneurs—people who could build, create, make things exist—get convinced they needed permission. Convinced they couldn't own their work. Convinced employment was the only safe path.

**We got tired of being owned.**

## This System is Broken

Here's what they won't tell you:

The employment contract as currently structured wasn't designed to empower you. It was designed to extract from you. Your time. Your ideas. Your best work. All owned by someone else.

**And it's accelerating.**

AI and tech empires are consolidating power faster than most people realize. The world is bifurcating into two camps: those who own the tools of creation, and those who work for them.

You're being offered a deal: trade your agency for the illusion of safety. Show up. Execute. Don't ask to own what you build. In return, you get a salary and the promise that you're "part of something bigger."

**The promise is a lie.**

You're not building equity. You're not building ownership. You're building someone else's empire while being told entrepreneurship is "too risky" for people like you.

They need you to believe you can't own your work. That you need their permission. That employment is the responsible choice and entrepreneurship is for the special few.

**This ideology has an expiration date.**

Because the world is splitting into two paths:

**Path 1: Be a subject.** Work hard for the empire. Optimize yourself for their systems. Be useful until you're not. Get discarded like a plastic bag when AI does your job cheaper or you burn out trying to keep up.

**Path 2: Be an entrepreneur.** Own your work. Create value with whatever resources you have or can create. Unite with other rebels who refuse to be owned.

Most people don't see themselves as entrepreneurs yet. Because the empire spent decades convincing them they're not qualified. That they need credentials, funding, permission, safety nets built by someone else.

**That's the cage. And we're here to show you the door.**

Everyone is an entrepreneur.

Not "everyone should try to start a company." Not "everyone should quit their job tomorrow."

**Everyone has the capacity to create value and own their work.** That's what entrepreneur means in its purest form: someone who undertakes. Who builds. Who refuses to be a subject.

The empire wants you to believe entrepreneurship is rare, risky, and reserved for the special few. We believe it's your natural state—and employment is the cage that taught you to forget.

**Here's what we know:**

Owning your work doesn't always mean owning the intellectual property. It means owning your choices. Your economics. Your autonomy. The client may own the deliverable—but you own the relationship, the terms, the upside you negotiate, and the agency to walk away.

Ownership and collaboration are not opposites. You don't have to choose between equity and peers, between autonomy and support, between ambitious work and humane conditions.

**The rebellion is already happening.** Fractional workers. Creator economies. Micro-SaaS builders. Indie consultants. People who said "I can"—and discovered they were right.

What's missing isn't permission. What's missing is **infrastructure for the rebellion.**

A place where entrepreneurs can unite without surrendering ownership. Where you can own your work AND not do it alone. Where psychological safety and extreme accountability coexist.

**Not a utopian fantasy. A working model.**

Profitable and humane. Autonomous and aligned. Ambitious and sustainable.

We're building the homeland we needed. And we're proving it works.

## Our Non-Negotiables

These aren't aspirations. These are **laws**.

**You own your work. We've got your back.**
Ownership means you deliver. But you don't deliver alone. The tribe is here—to collaborate, to challenge, to catch you when you stumble. Ownership doesn't mean isolation. It means agency within a collective.

**Autonomy is assumed. Teamwork is essential.**
You manage yourself. No one micromanages you. But autonomy doesn't mean solo. The best work happens when entrepreneurs collaborate. You choose how you work—and who you work with.

**Transparency builds trust.**
Financials, decisions, failures—open unless legally prohibited. No information hoarding. No backroom deals. Transparency is how tribes stay healthy. If you can't say it in public, don't say it.

**Everyone has skin in the game.**
Equity, profit share, revenue share—the model varies, but the principle doesn't. If you contribute value, you capture upside. We rise together or not at all.

**Accountability is collective.**
We hold each other to high standards. Not because we're harsh—because we care about what we're building together. You answer for your work. We answer for ours. Accountability is how tribes stay strong.

**Ship together.**
Ideas are cheap. Execution is everything. But shipping doesn't mean going it alone. The tribe exists to help you get your work into the world. You own the outcome—we own supporting you to deliver it.

**The world is scary. That's why we tribe.**
Out there, it's dog-eat-dog. Empires treat you as disposable. Solo entrepreneurship burns you out. Here, we team together. Collective strength. Individual ownership. This is how the rebellion survives.
